Brief sketch of two Ilastik applications: online optogenetic activation and 3D tissue segmentation.

Optogenetics allow for the precise spatiotemporal control of cellular processes. Within a tissue, a local photoactivation is achieved through the definition of ROIs that enclose a group of cells but remain fixed throughout the experiment. In my talk I will describe a method by which ROIs are dynamically adjusted as cells rearrange in response to optogenetic activation. I will also describe an approach to 3D segmentation of tissue in which cells would be merged using other approaches.

METHODS SUMMARY

Cloning

To generate RhoGEF2-Cry2::mCherry, the DH-PH catalytic domain of RhoGEF2 was PCR-amplified from Drosophila melanogaster cDNA using gene-specific primers and fused through overlap extension PCR to Cry2::mCherry from a plasmid kindly provided by Mathieu Coppey. A non-fluorescently-tagged RhoGEF2-Cry2 was also cloned by amplifying RhoGEF2::Cry2 from RhoGEF2-Cry2::mCherry. Both constructs were cloned into the pPW vector (Drosophila Genomics Resource Center, Bloomington, IN) using the Gateway cloning system (Life Technologies) according to standard procedures.
