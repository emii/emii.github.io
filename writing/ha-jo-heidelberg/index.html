Ha-jo! Heidelberg

Old bridge, Heidelberg October 2013

Ha-jo! is the Narrenruff in Heidelberg. This celebration calling is shouted out loud during the street parade and the Fastnacht (carnival) festivities to demonstrate an expression of a joyfull spirit

There is nothing special, or there might not be anything special about Heidelberg. It might very well be, that the city, and its ihabitants and whatever that surrounds the city, makes up for just another good example of a german university town. However, for me Heidelberg happens to be a very special place. I like to believe that the city is composed of a well balanced mixture of many small but enjoyable details. Each of those tiny pieces arranged in such a configuration that makes it here very livable.

Its geography with hills, the river and the forest around the corner

it’s history and a university with its traditions There are various things which I very much appreciate a

I’ve spent here the past eight years and very meaningful moments

I do get to miss Heidelberg in every opportunity I happend to travel to other places in Europe.

Castle, Heidelberg October 2013

Few places are like Heidelberg

It’s been almost 8 years since I arrived to Heidelberg. Here I studied a Master and a PhD. Here I met a lot of my current friends. Here I learned and embraced both.

Every time I go and visit other places I end up missing Heidelberg and I’m always really happy when I return. It has become a good reference to me to assess how much I’d be willing to move.

Heidelberg is not only the city but also it’s sorroundings.

Here it feels like home. Soon I’ll be leaving, and it’s difficult not to think how much I’m going to miss this city.

What else can I say about Hiedelberg? For me it has been an amazing time living in this city.

Heiligenberg, Heidelberg October 2013
